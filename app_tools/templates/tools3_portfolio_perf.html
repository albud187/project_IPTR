Porfolio Backtest Plot



This tool plots historical performance of the input portfolio, both levered and unlevered as well as a benchmark index between the start and end dates.

Portfolio Holdings: list of tickers and their percent of capital allocation in the form ticker1:allocation1,ticker2:allocation2,ticker3:allocation3... (no space). Example: SPY:30,QQQ:35,IWM:25,GDX:10

Start/End Date: Date in format YYYY,MM,DD

Cash: How much of actual cash (not borrowed) is invested. Enter in numerical format, no dollar signs. For example: 1230.59.

Leverage: Ratio of (Cash + borrowed money)/(Cash). For example, if I invest $1500, using $1000 of my own cash and borrowing $500, that is a leverage of 1.5.

Interest Rate: Annual interest rate to borrow money expressed as a decimal.
Example: 0.015 corresponds to 1.5% interest rate.

Benchmark: A stock to which the portfolio will be compared. While you can use any ticker, it is reccomended to use an index fund such as SPY.
